7 Places To Visit in San Francisco if you're a tourist

San Francisco is one of the most famous cities in the world and is truly a remarkable sight. This list contains places that every tourist must experience from famous landmarks,shopping centers, world famous chocolate shops, riding cable cars, and seeing nature-esque sites. All of these places in San Francisco is what makes the city so unique, bringing in millions of visitors from around the world into this wondrous city of a gem.

California Academy of Sciences

55 Music Concourse Dr
San Francisco, CA
94118
(415) 379-8000

Visit California Academy of Sciences' website website

California Academy of Sciences is such an amazing, educational, and immersive experience. You can visit a large aquarium that is home to so many varieties of fish, to seeing the Planetarium, to exploring the Osher rainforest with amazing butterflies and birds, to looking at a coral reef, and looking at species of bugs, and so much more! It truly is a unique site to visit whether you love science or not as you can even go on top of the building and see what's called a "living roof" which houses an oasis of solar panels where all types of wildlife live.You encounter so many different types of animals and wildlife that has many different types of exhbitis that makes you want to stay there for hours and explore.

Golden Gate Bridge

Golden Gate Bridge
San Francisco, CA
94901
(415) 921-5858

Visit Golden Gate Bridge's website

One of the most iconic symbols of California is the Golden Gate Bridge. Walking on the bridge, not only can you see the vibrant red color of the bridge, but you can also see the beautiful view of city with the skyscrapers, ocean, and the mountains. The nature-esque scenery is so breathtaking and its towering presence makes you feel at awe for its architecture and beauty.

Alcatraz Island

San Francisco, CA
94133
(415) 561-4900

Visit Alcatraz Island's website

Alcatraz Island is an infamous prison in San Francisco that is on a small island. You would take the ferry to get to the island and had housed some of the most notorious criminals such as Al Capone and George Kelly. The prison is an attraction now and from the outside gives you a gorgeous view of the city. From the inside, you have a chance to explore what the prison looks like from its intimidating prison cells to its lighthouse, and to its military fort. Being able to visit a maximum security prison that was established in the 1800's is a pretty awesome experience to get to see historically and being able to ride in a ferry on the way there is pretty awesome too .

Pier 39

The Embarcadero
San Francisco, CA
94133
(415) 705-5500

Visit Pier 39's website

Pier 39 is truly one of the most unique spots in San Francisco. There are so many fun attractions to explore such as the Aquarium of the Bay, bunjee jumping, sailing, visiting small shops, watching sea lions on the port, eating amazing crepes, and so much more! The pier is known for its awesome views of the Golden Gate Bridge and the sea lions, as well as artists showing off their paintings on the decks of the pier..

Ghirardelli Square

900 North Point St.
San Francisco, CA
94109
(415) 775-5500

Visit Ghirardelli Square's website

Ghiradelli Square is a classic landmark here. It is home to one of the best tasting gourmet chocolate shops and from the outside is really pretty look at with its brick buildings. There are pretty lavish restaurants and shops, but most importantly, the Ghirardelli Chocolate Factory is where you can actually see the chocolate being made and order some super great sundaes filled with whipped cream and all kinds of syrups. If you have a sweet tooth for chocolate, then this is your place to explore.

San Francisco Botanical Garden

1199 9th Ave.
San Francisco, CA
94122
(415) 661-1316

Visit San Francisco Botanical Garden's website

The San Francisco Botanical Garden is home to many extraordinary plants and flowers from around the world. The naturesque beauty is so peaceful to look at and some of the best features of the garden is the Magnolia species, cloud forests from the South America region, and native plants from around the world. You can also visit the Library of Horticulture which has volumes of horticulture and gardening, and you can also visit the Ancient Plants Garden which is one of the coolest places you'll see. It houses exotic and prehistoric plants from the time of the dinoasaurs and huge tree ferns and palms.

Powell and Market Cable Car Kiosk

1 Powell St.
San Francisco, CA
94102
(415) 701-2311

Visit Powell and Market Cable Car Kiosk's website

One of the classic symbols of San Francisco is getting to ride on a cable car. The kiosk is the place where you can buy a ticket and ride on these cable cars that take you to some of the famous parts of San Francisco such as Fisherman's Wharf which houses Pier 39 or Union Square which is where the main shopping, theater, and hotels are. The best part about going on these is getting to stick your head out and watch the view or standing outside the car and holding onto the rail to see the city. The thrill of the ride makes it even more unique to explore SF in this way.
