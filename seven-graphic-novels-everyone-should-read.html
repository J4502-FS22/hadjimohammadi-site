7 graphic novels/comic books everyone should read

This list incorporates graphic novels/comic books that everyone should read once in their lifetime and is a mix of different themes/subjects, such as antisemitism, oppression, suicide, depression, mystery, adventure, and laughter. The purpose of this list is to display graphic novels that highlight important themes and showcase emotional moments through visual storytelling.

Based on True Events

Maus

By Art Spiegelman

Get "Maus" on Amazon.com

Maus is an emotional comic that tells the true story of Art Spiegelman's parents' survival in the Holocause in Poland. He draws the characters metaphorically as animals and the comic is like a documentary and addresses a serious topic that shows the reader how Holocaus survivors have to deal with pysychological and emotional trauma in a creative art form. Through visual storytelling, you gain an educational perspetive on seeing how people were treated during the Holocaust and makes you think critically about themes of anger, surivival, family, love, and grief.

Persepolis

By Marjane Sartrapi

Get "Persepolis" on Amazon.com

Persepolis is a black and white comic strip based on the author's personal experiences of growing up in Iran during the Islamic Revolution. It depicts her childhood until her early adult years and is important in educating readers. It gives you a glimpse of how life can be like under a repressive regime and shows you how different life can be for other people with powerful illustrations that add to the story.

Fun Home

By Alison Bechdel

Get "Fun Home" on Amazon.com

Funhome is a graphic memoir that traces Bechdel's life as a young girl to early adulthood, coming to understand about her sexuality but also builds her story around her father's death.The book explores the complicated dynamics of Bechdel s relationship with her father who is a closeted man. Themes of gender, sexuality, death, and family dysfunction play a role in the book and is a difficult story to read due to these themes. However, it is moving, intimate, funny, and raw, with unique illustrations.

Smile

By Raina Telgemeier

Get "Smile" on Amazon.com

Smile is a graphic memoir that tells the story of a girl named Raina who has to get braces due to an accidenr that knocks out her two front teeth. In the midst of getting surgery and braces, she also tackles on middle/high school, first love, unhealthy friendships, and headgear. It's a relatable story that tackles on these different themes that we've porbably all experienced in our lives and is also enjoyable. It features a complex protagonist who deals with a lot of insecurities but teaches you that it doesn't matter what you look like on the outside.

This One Summer

By Jillian Tamaki and Mariko Tamaki

Get "This One Summer" on Amazon.com

This book is a coming of age story about two teenage friends named Rose and Windy, and their summer in a small beach town. The novel tackles on teen pregnancy, Rose's mother's depression, suicide, family difficulties, and love. The book explores the characters' discovering their sexualities and identities while battling complicated family dynamics, and is just one of those books that teaches you the harsh realities of life at a young age.

Not Based on True Events

Watchmen

By Alan Moore

Get "Watchmen" on Amazon.com

Watchmen takes place in a dystopian universe where costumed superheroes exist and the U.S. had won the Vietnam War. It shows the moral struggles of superheroes Rorschach, Doctor Manhattan, the Nite Owl,Sally Jupiter, and others. They come together to figure out when one of their former comrades is killed and try to figure out who is killing superheroes. The story challenges the morality of superheroes and moral ambiguities to what it means to be a hero and the lengths you go to protect society. It provides very artistic and detailed illustrations that really make you feel like you're a part of the story and shows a depth in what it means to be a superheroe, with complex characters questioning themselves and their mission.

Tintin: The Crab with the Golden Claws

By Hergé

Get "Tintin: The Crab with the Golden Claws" on Amazon.com

Tintin is a story told in multiple books about a young reporter named Tintin who becomes involved in dangerous cases where he tries to save the day. This story tackles on Tintin dealing with international opium smugglers who have hidden the drug in tins of crab meat. With his sidekick dog Snowy and his friend Captain Haddock, Tintin is a comic filled with laughter, fighting for justice, and shows really colorful artwork that engages with the reader. When you read one, you want to continue reading on to the next book, eager to explore heroic characters, lighthearted humor, strong morality, and great coloring. It's a lighthearted story that can give you a break from themore serious comics and just makes you on the edge of your seat with featuring spies, assassins, drug lords, treasure hunters, and many more characters.
